Abstract:

Text-to-Speech (TTS) synthesis often face the ``one-to-many`` problem, where a single text input can result in multiple speech outputs with varying prosody due to insufficient prosodic information. Previous TTS models have attempted to extract prosodic information from text and speech but have struggled to generate speech with appropriate prosody for specific contexts. To address this challenge, this study explores the use of visual features to extract prosodic information, drawing inspiration from the successful application of visual features in speech synthesis, although not specifically for prosody. The findings demonstrate that visual features provide prosodic information that is not available from the text input alone, and their integration improves the performance of acoustic feature prediction. Our proposed model, VisualSpeech, incorporates both the visual and textual information, and the results demonstrate that the visual features help improve the naturalness of synthesized speech. This work highlights the potential of visual features in enhancing TTS systems, particularly from the perspective of prosody.
